Project

Labor Creates

Artist Statement

Labor Creates comes directly from the streets. To me, the phrase represents an understanding of the New York City hustle where time is money and what it takes to build a life through work.

Coming from a Caribbean immigrant family, I understand the drive and what it takes to survive in New York City. This series, photographed around Chinatown and Canal Street in Lower Manhattan, reflects my own experiences and emotions around living and working in the city. I have worked as a carpenter, electrician, Zamboni driver, app-based delivery worker, and an independent contractor.

These photographs acknowledge the pride of immigrant communities, a hard day’s work and the people whose goods, services, and labor keep New York City alive.
